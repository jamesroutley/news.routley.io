PhD gets 5x increase in postdoc interview invitations by just changing his name

So I did an experiment, I am looking for a postdoctoral position and decided to check to what extent racism in science could be. I took my CV and changed the name to a more western one. I'd send it out with my real name, then a few days later (or before) with the western name.

Out of 100, my applications with my real name got 17responses (all of them were negative). Out of 100, my applications with the western name got 87 responses (with 54 scientists willing to apply for a fellowship opportunity with “me”).

The results themselves were not surprising to me. I was more surprised to encounter harassment from researchers constantly advocating for equality, inclusion, and diversity.

One of the messages that I received was: “If we can keep lowering the barrier for entry, science will become a joke”. I close my case here!!
