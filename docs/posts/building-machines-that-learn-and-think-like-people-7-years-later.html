"Building Machines That Learn and Think Like People", 7 Years Later

In the post about GPT-4V, I mentioned the paper “Building Machines That Learn and Think Like People” (https://arxiv.org/abs/1604.00289) by Brenden Lake, Tomer Ullman, Joshua Tenenbaum, and Samuel Gershman. In that seminal paper the authors suggested that truly human-like learning and thinking machines will have to reach beyond current engineering trends in both what they learn, and how they learn it.

There was a famous example of wrong image captioning (Figure 6 of the original paper, reproduced below), when a captioning model had no right causal model, and even if understanding the key objects in a scene correctly, it failed to understand the physical forces at work, the mental states of the people, or the causal relationships between the objects. The captions were funny:

The original captions seven years ago was generated by the NeuralTalk2 image captioning model by Andrey Karpathy (https://github.com/karpathy/neuraltalk2). With GPT-4V finally available it would be strange not to try this again, with the new capabilities at hand.

The new captions by GPT-4V are really good! My prompt was just “Describe the image”.

The first image was a Wild West scene:

GPT-4V produced the following description:

“The image portrays a scene reminiscent of the Wild West. A dirt-covered street is flanked by wooden buildings, including a two-story structure with a sign reading "Last Chance Saloon." On the street, a man wearing a hat is riding a brown horse at a gallop. Unusually, he appears to be dragging another man behind the horse using a rope or lasso. This person is being pulled along the ground, creating a trail of dust behind him. There are bystanders observing the scene from the sidewalks and porches of the buildings. In the background, there are mountains, adding to the rugged setting of the scene.”

I want nothing to add. Seven years ago the NeuralTalk2 model gave the description “a woman riding a horse on a dirt road”.

The second image was an unusual situation with a plane:

What GPT-4V produced:

“The image captures a highly dramatic scene. A large airplane with propellers is seen flying extremely close to a bridge. The plane is at such a low altitude that its wing is almost touching the bridge's surface. The fuselage of the plane is oriented diagonally, suggesting that it might be in the midst of a descent or crash. The plane's propellers are clearly visible, indicating it's a propeller-driven aircraft. On the bridge, there is a taxi or a yellow car moving, seemingly unaware or unable to react to the looming plane. In the background, there are tall buildings and city structures, indicating this event is occurring in an urban area. The overall atmosphere of the image is intense, suggesting a moment of emergency or unexpected occurrence.”

The only thing I disagree is that the yellow taxi is likely deeply aware of the situation and stopped in emergency (the stop lights are on, the car is tilted to the right). But anyway, the description is good. Remember, the caption seven years ago was “an airplane is parked on the tarmac at an airport”.

The final image contains a natural disaster scene:

The GPT-4V was very wordy:

“The image depicts a distressing situation. Three men are seen wading through turbulent waters that appear to have flooded a street or coastal area. The man in the front is wearing a black shirt and orange shorts, and he seems to be leading the way, with a focused and urgent expression on his face. The second man, wearing a blue jacket, is carrying a large, orange lifejacket or flotation device on his back. The third man, dressed in a yellow raincoat, follows closely behind them.

Adding to the intensity of the scene, a white house is shown on its side, partially submerged in the water, suggesting it has been uprooted or knocked over by the force of the waters. Its windows and the facade suggest severe damage. There are also other structures and debris floating in the water, including parts of a wooden structure and a vehicle, further indicating the devastation of the area.

The overall atmosphere of the image conveys a sense of urgency, danger, and the destructive power of natural disasters. The cloudy and misty background adds to the sense of chaos and unpredictability.“

It’s so far from “a group of people standing on top of a beach“ seven years ago… The progress we have is really mindblowing.

It would be interesting to have a more thorough reassessment of the statements from the original paper.
