Paper map sales are booming

By Kate Morgan

Jan. 19, 2023 4:50 pm ET

IN THE 1880s, Iraqi archaeologist Hormuzd Rassam found a small carved tablet with an image of Babylon at its center, surrounded by a vast salt sea. Historians consider this tablet, roughly 2,500 years old, the earliest existing map of the world.

Even “modern” maps look quaint today, when apps like Google Maps put practically every intersection in the world a click away. We rely heavily on this innovation. One 2022 survey by tire retailer United Tires, found that, in the 20 U.S. cities with the most cars per capita, 93% of drivers depend on GPS to get around.
