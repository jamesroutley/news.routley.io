The way I think about it is that AI producers will increase the value of being able to do a few things that few others can (invent a much-loved product) and reduce the value of being able to do a lot of things that many other people can (solve 10 Leetcode problems in an hour).

I wish coders who build tools and APIs cared equally about their users. One of the bad aspects of open source culture is that market forces are not so hard at work, so incentives are not as aligned with providing a comfortable experience.

GitHub Issues does a decent job at mimicking market forces.
