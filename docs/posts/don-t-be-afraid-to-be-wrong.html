Don't be afraid to be wrong

Context

Being afraid can be helpful in certain situations but not all. Today, I am going to go over a few situations and provide techniques on how to prevent being wrong, and feeling (too) bad after being wrong; I am also going to discuss how to recover after being wrong, how to tell others you were wrong, and finally how to approach someone that is wrong.

Being afraid

Let's start with the "being afraid" part. We can all agree that being afraid is natural and often times useful but that it can be harmful if you get the risk-reward wrong.

Being wrong

In almost all (workplace) situations, it is not worth being afraid of being wrong.

You are wrong, now what?

The main thing to remember is that being wrong isn't the end of the world. Most likely everyone has already forgotten you got something wrong. The feeling you're having right after being wrong will fade away. With some practice, you will be able to recover from being wrong faster and faster.

Someone is wrong

If someone is wrong, it is important to remember that they are not wrong on purpose. Or at least that's how I approach thinking about it.
