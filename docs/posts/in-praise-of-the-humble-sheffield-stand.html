In praise of the humble Sheffield stand

In praise of the humble Sheffield stand: the best, most practical & most efficient bike parking solution. Simple, cheap, easy to install & suitable for the vast majority of bikes, allowing riders to easily lock both wheels & frame. Most alternatives are crap: here’s why: 1/n

Designers have long tried to improve on the Sheffield Stand: but why? Why create something more complex, harder to use, more difficult to install & more expensive? Like this. WTF is this? Why would you do this? Only one locking point & the other bike will get in your way. 2/n

Don’t get me started on this shit. These “wheel benders” are often found outside Wetherspoons & retail developments which want to tick the cycling box without being serious. You can’t securely lock your bike to these, you risk damage, they can fcuk off & die for all I care. 3/n

Believe it or not, there’s an alternative to the “wheel benders” which is even worse. Kill it with fire. 4/n

Someone has actually tried to improve on the “wheel bender” design failure and come up with this. No. Just no. 5/n

How about something a bit simpler where you just have to install one and it holds lots of bikes? As you can see, this one is a massive success. 6/n

On a similar theme, what about one unit you install and it holds lots of bikes? Not bad, but still not good enough imo: only one locking point and not big enough for a cargo bike or other non-standard ride. 7/n

This was designed by someone on LSD and installed by someone who wants your bike to get crushed by a tram. 8/n

This one was designed by David Byrne of Talking Heads. I like the idea, but notice how someone has chosen to lock their bike to a lamppost instead. Might get to use this once in a lifetime. 9/n

This one is a good for a primary school. If only like three kids ride to school. 10/n

Sexy? No. Doesn’t pass the simplicity test and completely unnecessary. 11/n

What about this? Simple, cheap, compact. It’s ok but probably a bit small imo. Is it better than a Sheffield stand? No. Has it easily been lifted out of the paving? Yes. 13/n

You see quite a lot of these around. Looks nice, simple, two locking points, but it still only works properly if the geometry fits your bike. Again, is it better than a Sheffield stand? No. 14/n

How about something more contemporary? A bit snazzy? This is not bad, I like the design but again it only works for bikes with a specific geometry. Good effort but still overly fussy. 15/n

What about these? You sometimes find them as an add-on attached to lampposts. They’re ok for short term parking but a bit small for locking both wheels comfortably. These ones have been positioned by a sadist who wants your wheel to extend into the bike lane. 16/n

These ones recently posted by

are a triumph of aesthetics over practicality. Kind of look cool from a certain angle but completely useless. Not a single bike parked here. Might as well twist up some paperclips and call them bike stands. 17/n

Ooh this looks nice. Is it any good? No. 18/n

These ones are fine: variations on the Sheffield stand. The brace-reinforced one is useful as it has an additional locking point & provides a bit of security if the stand becomes unattached from the ground. Curved one might cause problems for a few bikes but basically ok. 19/n

And that my friends is why the Sheffield stand is best, imo. Please try to resist some of the sub-standard crap here if you see it proposed for developments near you. Any other good / bad versions? Please post them below. 20/20
