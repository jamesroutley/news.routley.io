Stop selling “unlimited”, when you mean “until we change our minds”

Bottom line: Anthropic just added weekly limits to Claude Pro ($20/month) and Claude Max ($200/month), affecting their heaviest users of Claude Code…mid-workflow.

Why it matters: This is the same playbook we’ve seen AI companies use before—hook users with "unlimited" access, then cap the power users who matter most.

Here's how the AI pricing bait-and-switch works:

  1. Launch with generous/unlimited limits

  2. Build user dependency

  3. Add caps targeting "less than 5%" of users

  4. Frame as "sustainability" or "fairness"

Sound familiar? That's because we've seen this movie before. Cursor did it. Windsurf followed. GitHub Copilot tightened screws. Now Claude Code.

The thing is, Anthropic isn't wrong about the economics. Running Claude Sonnet or Opus models costs real money—we're talking about serious compute infrastructure here. But that's precisely why the bait-and-switch approach is so damaging to the developer community.

Who actually gets hit by these limits:

  • Power users with deep workflow integration

  • Early adopters who took platform risks

  • Team influencers who drive organizational adoption

  • $200/month Claude Max subscribers doing serious work

The real damage: You're not frustrating 5% of users—you're breaking trust with the exact people who drive growth and adoption.

Every bait-and-switch makes developers hedge bets instead of fully committing. Or maybe, at “best” they delay AI adoption, waiting for "stable" pricing. Or perhaps worst of all…self-limit usage to avoid hitting caps.

And on HackerNews, wowfunhappy observed:

I'm probably not going to hit the weekly limit, but it makes me nervous that the limit is weekly as opposed to every 36 hours or something. If I do hit the limit, that's it for the entire week—a long time to be without a tool I've grown accustomed to!

When developers get "rate limit exceeded" while debugging at 2 AM, they're not thinking about your infrastructure costs—they're shopping for alternatives.

We all know AI inference costs money. Developers get resource constraints…we have them ourselves!

What we don't get: Being sold "unlimited" and then discovering asterisks after we've built workflows around your tool.

What we want: Transparent costs upfront so we can make informed decisions.
