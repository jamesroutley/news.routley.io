Colosseum – A quick overview of the most powerful RF emulator

SignalProcessingJobs.com - The #1 job board and blog for signal processing engineers*

Originally created by the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) for a unique project called the Spectrum Collaboration Challenge (SC2), Colosseum is the world’s largest RF emulator. SC2 ran from roughly 2014-2019 where teams faced off to create robust modems and artificial intelligence algorithms that could maintain a link in a hostile or highly contested environment. The winner of the event took home 2 million dollars.

The event was interesting, but the centerpiece of it all, Colosseum, is even more interesting. To quote its brochure, its current specs are boggling:

  • 256 Software Defined Radios USRPs X310
  • 900 TB of Network Attached Storage (NAS)
  • 171 high-performance servers
  • 19 clock distribution systems
  • Software-based traffic generation solutions
  • hosted on a pool of dedicated servers
  • Full-mesh networking capability

During operation the testbed can pull some insane figures:

  • 3 Peta-Ops per second of computing power
  • 52 terabytes per second of data to emulate 65,000 channel operations between 256 wireless devices.
  • It can draw up to 92 kilowatts of power and requires 200 gallons of water per minute to cycle through its cooling system to keep it from overheating.
