Serious flaws in the way Samsung phones encrypt key material in TrustZone

Ugh god. Serious flaws in the way Samsung phones encrypt key material in TrustZone and it’s embarrassingly bad. They used a single key and allowed IV re-use. https://eprint.iacr.org/2022/208

So they could have derived a different key-wrapping key for each key they protect. But instead Samsung basically doesn’t. Then they allow the app-layer code to pick encryption IVs. This allows trivial decryption.

It’s not broken in every version. But of course there’s a downgrade attack.

I always wonder why academic researchers end up being the ones who find and repair these problems for huge companies and their 100s of millions of users.

There is always pushback in the security research community against doing “attack work” that finds these vulnerabilities. Which is reasonable but too bad, since the alternative is Samsung farting your secret keys everywhere.

(I’m not saying that academic researchers should be the first line of defense. They should be the last one. Like Batman, or the A-Team.)
