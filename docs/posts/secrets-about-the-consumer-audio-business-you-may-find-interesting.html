  • #17
  • Most Chinese factories and engineers are perfectly capable of producing high-end equipment... but their customers don't ask for it.
  • Packaging costs are probably a bigger factor than you think.
    • There are undoubtedly mid-low-end IEMs where the package costs more than the IEM. In fact it's not even hard to do this. You can source a low-end IEM for $2 and put it in a $3 box without going out of your way. It goes on Amazon for $20 and oddly enough everyone seems happy.

Fun fact. I asked for a test sample to a Canadian packaging manufacturer, a small cardboard box. The quote was 5 times what I was getting from a Chinese shop, but not only that the edges weren't ninety degree, there was folding gaps, glue spills, it looked terrible. When I mentioned the flaws, the answer I got was: "Well you can't expect the same quality than China". I was stoked, I already knew not to expect them to compete in price, but even in quality, there was simply no such expertise and technology locally even if I was prepared to pay multiple times more.

  • #18

Oh boy, the whitegoods market is an extreme example! There are really only 5 main companies making domestic appliances and own the vast majority of major brands. What people assume is a good brand doesn't really match with reality any more.

I spend almost 8 years at a major online retailer dealing with returns on whitegoods, so I have some insights in the actual numbers with a big sample size. In Europe the classic brands are still the best in terms of durability, at least within the 2 year warranty period. So that's BSH (Bosch & Siemens), Miele, Electrolux (AEG, Electrolux, Zanussi). We stopped selling certain types of products from specific brands (not the ones I mentioned) because they were just bad. So we would sell a washing machine or fridge from brand x, but wouldn't sell dishwashers from the same brand.
