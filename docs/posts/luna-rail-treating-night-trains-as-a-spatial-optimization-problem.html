Show HN: Luna Rail – treating night trains as a spatial optimization problem

Luna Rail is developing a new generation of night & day trains

optimizing space usage, comfort and economics all at once, providing a sustainable alternative for airline passengers and the best possible travel experience.

Travel with Comfort, without CO2

Night trains offer a sustainable alternative to aviation, at a fraction of the emissions per passenger-km.

The problem of existing night trains is lack of comfort, lack of privacy, that the trains have low capacity and can only run at night. This limits their attractiveness and economics. Our technology will solve those problems and will bring night trains into the future.

The Hotel Pods: affordable premium

The hotel pod offers a private single cabin for all passengers while, significantly increasing the capacity over conventional sleeper cars.

By day, a spacious sofa creates your personal living room or office on the move. At night, the seat smoothly converts into a large, comfortable bed.

There are also variants for two or three passengers.

The Seat Pods: full privacy with high capacity

The Seat Pod offers a large, private seat for daytime travel that converts into a full lie-flat bed for the night.

Our technology achieves a high capacity, comparable to a six-passenger couchette and approaching that of conventional day coaches. But we provide more space, direct aisle access, and true individual privacy for all.

Developed in the physical world

We believe that the user of a product and their experience should be the central for development. You can’t test ergonomics or comfort from a rendering alone. It must be experienced. That’s why our process has been accompanied by continuous user-tests in physical full-sized mock ups and rapid iterations based on the feedback we get.

With have run Tests with over hundreds of participants, each trying our various pods. Our “users” have communicated a high attractiveness of our technology both for day and night usage.

Guided by a Goal

Our development process is guided by a long-term, patent-pending goal: a day-and-night rail vehicle that maximizes passenger capacity. We adapted the technology for the real-world constraints of existing conventional railcars.

The approach is intended to make night trains truly competitive. We want to unlock the potential for profitable operations, lower ticket prices, and a significant expansion of the night train network.

Explore “our approach” page.

We have developed and tested our approach assuming a newly developed rail vehicle. We then worked “backwards” adapting the approach towards existing rail cars.

In the short and medium term, our technology involving cabins around a central corridor can significantly improve the efficiency of night trains. In the long term, our patented approach involving cabins on all sides of a central corridor will increase capacity by another 25%.
