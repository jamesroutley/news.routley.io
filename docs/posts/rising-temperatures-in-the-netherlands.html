Rising Temperatures in the Netherlands

Koen van Gilst / December 16, 2023

2 min read • ––– views

  • climate change
  • data visualization

Rarely does a day pass without some news of our changing climate. Here in the Netherlands, we're also experiencing this firsthand – our summers are hotter and our winters milder. In this blog post, I've tried to visually capture these changes. By using temperature data from the Dutch Meteorological Institute (KNMI), I've created two visualizations that show how the average temperature in The Netherlands has changed over the last 123 years.

Yearly temperature anomalies

The first visualization shows the yearly temperature anomalies in The Netherlands from 1901 to 2023. The data is based on the average temperature of each year, compared to the average temperature of the period 1901-2000 (previous century). The visualization is interactive, so you can hover over the graph to see the exact temperature anomaly for each year.

Heatmap of monthly temperature anomalies

The second visualization shows the monthly temperature anomalies in The Netherlands from 1901 to 2023. I'm using a heatmap to visualize the data, where the color of each cell represents the temperature anomaly for that month. So if you see a lot of red, it means that the temperature was higher than average; if it's blue, the temperature was lower than average.

The data is based on the average temperature of each month, compared to the average temperature of the period 1901-2000 (previous century). The visualization is interactive like the previous one.
