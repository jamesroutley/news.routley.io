Yak-Shaving for a Rubber Duck

I’m a professional programmer and a casual crocheter. A few years ago, I made the most idiomatic1 project possible for a programmer: a hoodie for a rubber duck, made from yak wool.

Why a duck?

There’s a debugging technique called rubber-duck debugging. My phrasing:

Put a rubber duck on your desk.

Go through your code step-by-step, explaining it in terms that the duck understands.

By the time you get to the end, you’ll realize why the code doesn’t work.

Of course, it doesn’t have to be a rubber duck: I’ll often use a journal or a peer instead of an inanimate object. But I still keep the duck on my desk.2

I use rubber-duck debugging for computer programs, but I hear from Edie that it also works for people programs.3

Why yak wool?

Another idiom in programming circles is yak-shaving:

The example I learned goes something like this:4

  • I need to take the bar exam, but it requires a Windows computer, and we only have Linux around the house.
  • To install Windows, I need an 8GB USB stick, but I don’t have one.
  • I could borrow one from Chris, but I had promised to knit his kid a sweater and haven’t, so it would be awkward to ask.
  • The kid is (somehow) allergic to acrylic and sheep wool and alpaca … but not yaks?
  • There isn’t yak wool for sale,5 but there is a farm nearby that will let me shave a yak for a reasonable amount of money.

So… I’m shaving a yak to take the bar exam.

There’s a nuance in the usage I’m familiar with: the yak-shaving is not strictly necessary for the original task. I don’t really need to shave the yak – I could just get a USB stick from the store – but shaving the yak lets me put off installing Windows.

Why a hoodie?

Standard hacker garb.

Why a yellow duck?

I didn’t spend a lot of time picking a color.6

I made the body and hood by freehand single-crochet in the round. I made the front pocket separately, and (clumsily) attached it after the fact.

According to my records,7 I used mYak Baby Yak Medium yarn, in the “Oatmeal” colorway. (That’s not an affiliate link; I have no relation with mYak.)
