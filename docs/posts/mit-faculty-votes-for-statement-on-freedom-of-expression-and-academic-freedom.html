MIT Faculty Votes for Statement on Freedom of Expression and Academic Freedom

CHRISTMAS COMES EARLY TO MASSACHUSETTS!

’s faculty votes to adopt the “MIT Statement on Freedom of Expression and Academic Freedom”!

The statement – inspired by the gold standard for campus free speech, the Chicago Statement – broadly commits the institute to cultivating an environment that encourages freedom of expression. /2

It reads in part, “With a tradition of celebrating provocative thinking, controversial views, and nonconformity, MIT unequivocally endorses the principles of freedom of expression and academic freedom.” /3

The statement urges all at MIT to research, learn, discuss, and debate ideas – controversial or mainstream – freely and openly. You can read the full statement below. /4

Note that while community members are urged to speak freely, the statement provides limited, measured exceptions, vowing to only prohibit “direct threats, harassment, plagiarism, or other speech that falls outside the boundaries of the First Amendment.” /5

MIT’s administration should follow suit and endorse the faculty’s statement to signal to the university community that MIT is a place where free expression thrives. Welcome to the club, MIT! /6
