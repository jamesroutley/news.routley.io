Maslow never proposed a pyramid

Your quarterly reminder that Maslow never proposed a pyramid and that the first visual of it was not in the psychology world, but in a management book from a business professor Keith Davis in 1957 and it looked like this.

“Maslow’s pyramid” is probably one of the most viral memes that the business world has invented and it emerged more than a decade after Maslow first wrote about a “hierarchy of needs” in 1943

The first pyramid was in business journalists article by Charles McDermid writing in Business Horizons

The conclusion of the article was that this might be a way to motivate employees without paying them more (which was not a normal idea at the time) Here is a quote from the piece

The interesting thing about Maslow’s 1943 paper was his doubt about the hierarchy (which he still proposed might be a hierarchy) Here’s what he said at the time

This laid the groundwork of what he really spent most of his time on over the next few decades. Trying to reshape the psychology profession around two frames D-psychology: focusing on deficits or lack B-psychology: focusing on thriving

This latter research never quite took off and some people think this is why Maslow never really pushed back on the pyramid. He liked the attention and felt like he never got enough credit for the other stuff. Todd bridgeman writes this here: https://journals.aom.org/doi/10.5465/amle.2017.0351…

The thing about Maslow is that even in that first paper he was so passionate about the “self-actualization” part of his hierarchy. He thought that it meant meeting the basic needs first but admitted he didn’t really know much about this.

Eventually he would go about meeting these people which he described as “Peakers”. He described them as people able to access different states. He differentiated non-peaking self actualizers as different than those optimizing around the “being needs”

My conclusion: Maslow would have loved the internet and I wish he had been around a bit longer. In 2016 here is what he wrote about how he saw the future of psychology I’m guessing he would have been disappointed by the current state of social research.

A better way to synthesize his research would be to look at two different states the b-realm and the d-realm. You can likely live in one or both. Many people start in the d-realm and may spend their whole lives solving for lack (of love, respect, connection, etc…)

This is why it’s sad that Maslow passed when he did. His later research is all over the place.
