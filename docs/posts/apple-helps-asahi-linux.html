Apple Helps Asahi Linux

Looks like Apple changed the requirements for Mach-O kernel files in 12.1, breaking our existing installation process... and they *also* added a raw image mode that will never break again and doesn't require Mach-Os. And people said they wouldn't help. This is intended for us.

Seriously, I can't think of a single reason why they'd add that for themselves. They build real Mach-Os with their own process. They have no use for raw images. They are saying "hey, use this, it's easier and we won't break it in the future". This is for Asahi.

Anyway, add `--raw --entry-point 34816 --lowest-virtual-address 16384` to your kmutil options to make it work on 12.1. This won't (yet) work with payloads, but does for raw m1n1. Need to make some changes for payloads.

Added a proper raw target that should work with payloads (and gets rid of the Mach-O truncation trick we were abusing earlier, which I was never confident would continue to work in the future anyway). Also this removes the payload size limit.

Also those people who say Apple will lock down the mac are wrong. Apple knows that the Mac is meant to stay open. Mac people are different than ios and ipados people.

It’s not about the people. Most Mac users use iPhone’s and many have iPads too. It’s about understanding the goals of each specific platform.
