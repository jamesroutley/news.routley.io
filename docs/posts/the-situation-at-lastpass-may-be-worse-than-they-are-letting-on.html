I think the situation at

may be worse than they are letting on. On Sunday the 18th, four of my wallets were compromised. The losses are not significant. Their seeds were kept, encrypted, in my lastpass vault, behind a 16 character password using all character types.

This password was never reused, and was generated using dice rolls. The same goes for the passwords needed to access the wallets locally; these passwords were also stored only in lastpass. Some of these wallets have not been accessed for months.

Leading up to this, there were no strange log in attempts on any of my accounts. I did not download anything. My machines are clean, and I have physical 2fa on everything. None of the links or contracts I interacted with were malicious. Nobody else had physical access to my PC.

In the space of 25 minutes, the attacker stole funds from 4 different chains, but did some very odd things: - Swapped tokens in my wallet before sending to themselves - Did not sweep a derived address - Missed several assets - Failed SOL transactions multiple times

All funds were sent to

, where I assume they were bridged to an anonymized chain. I learned of these events about 2 hours after they happened. While I had initially imagined I was targeted by a 0day or rootkit, I cannot find any evidence of this.

Having spoken to multiple professionals, it now seems far more likely that my encrypted vault was breached. However, at 100,100 PBKDF2 iterations, it is not realistic to brute force this. I think this situation goes far beyond what has been admitted.

If you use lastpass, immediately change all related passwords & find an alternative. I think it is important people are made aware of this breach.

If anyone has any more insight into this or feel they could help investigate it, please reach out.
