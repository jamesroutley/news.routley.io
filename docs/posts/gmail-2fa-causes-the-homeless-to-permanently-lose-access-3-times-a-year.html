Gmail 2FA causes the homeless to permanently lose access 3 times a year

Google's product designers should talk to my unhoused friends. When you require a mobile phone number for authentication, you guarantee that unhoused people will permanently lose access to their email when their phone is lost, stolen or destroyed (roughly every 12 weeks).

Unhoused people tend to get their phones through the "Obamaphone" program, which means that replacing a lost or stolen phone results in a completely new phone number. Service providers and caseworkers rely on email to communicate with unhoused folks. Huge disconnect here.

I've got about 30 unhoused neighbors whom I regularly communicate with. Not a single one has been able to keep the same cell phone number for more than 4 months at a time. It's usually 4 weeks or less. Text-based 2FA means they permanently lose access to their email accounts

"As a non-technical unhoused person who regularly, suddenly, and permanently loses access to their mobile phone number every 4 weeks, I would like to retain access to my GMail account SO THAT I can stay in touch with my case workers and obtain services and housing"

For the people wondering whether Google Voice would help — the answer is no. Google Voice itself requires (a) the ability to log into a Google account and (b) a non-GV mobile number linked to the account for verification purposes. Chicken-and-egg problem for an unhoused person.
