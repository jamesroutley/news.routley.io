Why you might want a domain-specific database like TigerBeetleDB

There've been a bunch of folks asking why you might want a domain-specific database like

(TB) There are a couple of reasons you might be interested in TB for accounting (Keep in mind TB has been in development for a few years and I'm still learning it) 1/x

1. Data modelling. Devs track payments in myriad ways. Maybe the safest way though is what accountants do: double-entry accounting. TB does this, giving you 2 entities: accounts and transfers. Insert transfers and query the accounts (if you want) for sums of credits/debits 2/x

2. Accounting-oriented business logic. TB comes with flags for things like rejecting transfers where debits would exceed credits on an account. It can also accept/reject *groups* of transfers. And more, along these lines. 3/x

5. Performance. Being dedicated to this one workload lets TB handle 1M transfers per second while all the time still handling business logic and validation of transfers. 6/x

6. Open source. TigerBeetle is Apache 2.0 and pretty dedicated to never going BSL. Open-source puts us in a healthy position to either become the underlying tech behind *everyone* doing payments or at least allows for us to lead in defining the protocols for this workload. 7/x

That's enough key concepts for now! It's still early days. Lots of work to do. Performance characteristics will change over time as we dev. There'll be bugs and we'll fix them. The user experience will get better. Hopefully though this thread gives you a sense of the direction!
