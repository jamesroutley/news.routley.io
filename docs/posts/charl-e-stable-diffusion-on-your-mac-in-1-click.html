Charl-e: “Stable Diffusion on your Mac in 1 click”

CHARL-E packages Stable Diffusion into a simple app. No complex setup, dependencies, or internet required — just download and say what you want to see.

M1 or M2 Mac required.

Write a prompt and watch it come to life *

All these images were created with Stable Diffusion. And now, you can run it yourself.

* Not literally

01Features

Features

Get Stable Diffusion running in minutes.

  1. Automatically Download Weights

    CHARL-E will automatically download weights for you, but you can also provide your own.

  2. Configure the Model

    If you want, configure your sampler (ddim or plms), image number, seeds, weights, and where to save your image. More options coming soon.

  3. Generate an Image

    You can generate an image with a single click. CHARL-E will automatically download the image for you. Nothing is sent to our servers and everything runs locally—no internet required.

  4. Gallery Mode

    See all your creations in one place. Click any image to see the full size version.

02Why CHARL-E?

CHARL-E is the easiest way to get started with Stable Diffusion.

  1. Super simple UI

    Enter a prompt and press enter, and then CHARL-E will do the rest.

  2. One click download

    No python scripts, no command line. Just one click and you’re ready to go.

  3. Private and offline

    Once you run the initial install, CHARL-E can run fully offline. Nothing is sent to our servers. Everything is open source on GitHub.

Frequently asked questions

If this doesn't help, reach out to me at choltz@hey.com.

What is Stable Diffusion?
The technical answer: it's a latent text-to-image diffusion model. The English answer: its a new AI model that lets you create images from natural language. If you've heard of DALL-E, it's like that but open source.

What's the difference between the two downloads? What is no weights?
The normal download contains "training weights" for the CHARL-E model to run. If you want a smaller download, install the no weights version and add your weights directory in the options menu.
