University of Michigan selling student speech and papers as LLM training data

The University of Michigan’s English Language Institute has created two English language corpora that are ideal for fine-tuning Large Language Models (LLMs).

1Academic Speech Dataset

Michigan Corpus of Academic Spoken English

The University of Michigan has recorded 65 speech events from a wide range of academic settings, including lectures, discussion sections, interviews, office hours, study groups, seminars and student presentations. Speakers represent broad demographics, including male and female and native and non-native English speakers from a wide variety of academic disciplines.

2Academic Papers Dataset

Michigan Corpus of Upper-Level Student Papers

University of Michigan has curated a corpus of high-quality, upper level student papers in a text database. The papers, written by U-M seniors and graduate students, all received “A” grades.
