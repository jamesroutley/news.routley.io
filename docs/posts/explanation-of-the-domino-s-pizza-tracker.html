Explanation of the Domino's Pizza tracker

A lot of folks saying it's fake, but that's not entirely accurate. I used to work as a manager at a Domino's Pizza. This was rolling out after I left, but my old GM let me make some pizzas while visiting my hometown a while back. It runs on a system called PULSE -

When the phone CS puts in the order, it goes to the prep line, then bake. That part is automated based on usual time. But the prepper can control it with the keyboard under their monitor. Depending on which order they're on, they can hit a key to mark it as prepped.

When the product is marked as prepped, the bake process timer is always going to be the same because the pizza ovens all work the same. They have a rolling metal sheet that you place the pizza on, and it pops out the other side when it's done. So the "bake" time is automated.

The quality check is just a guy cutting the pizza and putting it in the right box to put on the warmer ready for delivery or pick-up and marking it as ready. The delivery person checks pizzas out of the system to be delivered. This is so everyone knows who is taking what delivery

If everyone is working in sync and on time, then your tracker will be accurate. But if someone is trying to game the system to make corporate believe they get everything done in record time, they can control the system to fake the times.

This is why you'll see the next step is barely red, but doesn't start showing bold red until it's actually being prepped or out for delivery, etc. Employees do have to click on specific orders to put them into the processing line then mark them as completed and onto the next step

The times will always be accurate on slow days or when corporate is visiting a store, but because dominos puts so much marketing into speed, store employees sometimes mark things as done before actually getting done. They don't want to get in trouble for slow speeds.

Also, if your order gets messed up, there's no way to start the tracker again, so your order will be delayed even though it says it's ready. Always a good idea to check the boxes and make sure you have your sauces and drinks before leaving.
