California bill would require bots to disclose that they are bots

  • Requires all online bots and AI-powered accounts to disclose their automated nature in California.
  • Expands the definition of bots to include accounts using generative artificial intelligence.
  • Mandates immediate bot disclosure when asked by users during online interactions.
  • Requires clear and visible bot disclosures on all platforms with over 10 million monthly U.S. users.

Bill has been formally introduced and read for the first time in its house of origin (2/4/2025)

We're working on it! Check back later.

Assembly Member Wilson's bot disclosure legislation redefines California's approach to automated online communications by requiring all bots to identify themselves during interactions with state residents, regardless of their purpose. The measure expands current law, which only mandates disclosure when bots aim to influence commercial transactions or voting behavior.

The bill updates the legal definition of "bot" to encompass accounts operated by generative artificial intelligence - including systems that create synthetic images, videos, audio, and text. Under the proposed changes, any person using a bot must clearly disclose its automated nature during online interactions and provide this information when asked by other users. The disclosure requirements apply across public-facing websites, web applications, and digital platforms.

These modifications reflect the evolving landscape of artificial intelligence technology. The legislation establishes new parameters for transparency in online communications by requiring bot identification in all cases, not just those involving potential deception. Online platforms with over 10 million monthly U.S. visitors would need to ensure bot operators on their services comply with these expanded disclosure rules.
