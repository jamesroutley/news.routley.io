Juxtaposition: Capacity for Good or Evil

I -love- weird, ironic, or amusing juxtapositions.

Or juxtapositions that are beautiful through complementing or contrasting each-other… like two writers from different centuries who come to the same conclusion—but with language or stories that differ wildly… or like two images that seem to do nothing but contrast with each-other until you stare at them for a time.

Hamlet: “…I am very prowd, revengefull, ambitious, with more offences at my beck then I have thoughts to put them in, imagination to give them shape, or time to acte them in.”

—Shakespeare

He was so prolific in doing good that his biographers and friends noted that “he lacked time for half the good works in his mind”? and that “factories did not spring up more rapidly in Leeds and Manchester than schemes of benevolence beneath his roof.”

—“What's best next : how the gospel transforms the way you get things done,” by Matt Perlman
