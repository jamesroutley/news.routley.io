Social engineering GoDaddy (2007)

Back in 2007 my buddy [redacted] wanted to buy the domain name f*cktube.com as we were adult webmasters at the time. He was out of luck. A quick WHOIS search revealed that "f*cktube.com" had already been registered on GoDaddy. This was at a time when adult tube websites were getting big and independent website owners could still make untold sums of money — and he really wanted the domain.

He wanted to make the owner an offer but there was a problem. The owner's email address was hidden by GoDaddy's WHOIS protection. This.. simply wouldn't do.

We decided to hop on a call and 3way GoDaddy. He was to play the role of the baffled customer trying to retrieve only his email address so that he could reset his password — as he'd forgotten what name, address and email address he'd used when registering f*cktube.com.

I listened in for a few minutes of agonizing guff. They were getting nowhere. The support representative sounded like he wanted to help, but just couldn't because of QA restrictions. 

Silent until that point — I decided to employ the trusty line interruption method.

To begin, I sent 2 touch tones from my phone. *beep boop*

"Hey Bob, how are ya? Do you mind if I place the caller on a brief hold?"

I convinced the GoDaddy employee, "Bob," that I was a supervisor and that I'd monitored and interrupted his call with my accomplice to have a private conversation with him. I reassured him that I was monitoring the call and simultaneously doing research on the caller. I told Bob that he was clear to reveal the email address to the caller, and that I would now reconnect the call.

It worked. The GoDaddy employee spilled the email address of the domain owner to my accomplice.

If we wanted to be more malicious, I could have authorized the employee to update the email address and password on the account. We could have gone as far as stealing 2 and 3 character .com domain names all night, transferring them through a web of foreign registrars and ultimately selling them. I could have convinced the phone representatives to download trojans that gave us access to GoDaddy internal networks and ultimately tools. Hell, these days we'd just sell the domains we stole on ogusers for Monero — but we're too old for that now, and we were too cautious even then.

[redacted] emailed the owner of f*cktube.com and lowballed him. The plan worked. He developed it into a thriving adult tube site and made a fortune. Social engineering paid off.

The end.
