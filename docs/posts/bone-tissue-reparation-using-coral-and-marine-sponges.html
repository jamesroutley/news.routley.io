The Advantages

Â Â Â Â Â Â Â Â Â Â Â Â  Typical implants for this procedure, usually ceramics or alloys, prevent stress on the bone structure they are grafted to, a process known as stress-shielding. In this process, the much stronger metals or ceramics absorb all of the stress applied to the bone. This prevents bone, which uses stress as an indication to produce more bone cells, from healing and growing properly, causing the bone to atrophy and possibly die.[4] Coral and marine sponges, on the other hand, are not stronger than bone, and simply provide the framework for the bone cells to build around. They avoid the problem of stress-shielding.[4]
