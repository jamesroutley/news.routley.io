Built-in Support for Open-Source Components

As a POSIX compliant OS, QNX supports any software developed for POSIX Operating Systems, meaning that many open-source projects can be compiled for QNX with little or no modification. General FAQ

What is the purpose of this initiative?

This initiative aims to provide engineers and developers with easy access to QNX software and resources for non-commercial use, fostering innovation and skills development in the embedded systems community. We want to enable you to learn and develop for the QNX platform, empowering you to take the performance of your projects, and your career, to the next level.

Who can benefit from this initiative?

This initiative is ideal for hobbyists, students, industry professionals, and anyone interested in embedded systems for non-commercial purpose. Whether you're just starting out or looking to expand your expertise, our tools and resources can help you achieve your goals as a software developer.

Can I start developing on QNX SDP 8.0 for free?

Yes! QNX Everywhere has been designed to provide easy access to QNX software and resources for non-commercial use. Whether you're a hobbyist, student, or industry professional, you can quickly and easily get a QNX SDP 8.0 license as well as practical tools, comprehensive training, and a thriving community to support your embedded systems projects – all for free!

What does "non-commercial use" mean?

This license grants free access to QNX Software Development Platform 8.0 for individual non-commercial users. You can use it for educational purposes, personal projects, or any other activities that do not involve generating revenue or conducting business operations.

Non-commercial use includes academic environments, hobbyists, and those interested in learning about real-time operating systems. It does not cover evaluation or development for commercial purposes.

Please review the terms and conditions of the QNX Development License - Non-Commercial License (QDL) for Software Development Platform 8.0.

For commercial 30-day evaluation licenses, apply here.

Is QNX SDP 7.1 available as part of this initiative?

No. QNX SDP 8.0 is tailor-made for next-generation software platforms with SoCs that have four or more CPU cores. We want the next-generation of developers and hobbyists to start their journey on the best possible footing, with the latest software development tools we have to offer.

Troubleshooting FAQs

I registered for a new myQNX account. Something went wrong. I can’t log in. How can I get help?

Where is the product license I requested?

You can view licenses and accept them via myQNX License Manager. Before you begin using the QNX Software Center, visit your myQNX account profile to view the products that have been deployed to you. The profile includes a Registered Products section, which shows at a glance the license key, serial number, license certificate, and license administrators for each deployed product.

Instructions on license management, allocation, and deployment are provided in the myQNX License Manager and QNX Software Center User's Guide. If the license does not exist in your myQNX License Manager, please contact us at myqnx@blackberry.com.

When I try to download QNX Software Center, it says that I need to “Register” and asks for a Product Serial Number and Password. Where can I find this information?

In order to download QNX Software Center, you (myQNX user) need to have a QNX product license assigned. Instructions on license management, allocation, deployment are provided in the myQNX License Manager and QNX Software Center User's Guide.
