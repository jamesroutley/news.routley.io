Wikipedians are rebelling against “unethical” Wikipedia fundraising banners

Wikipedians are rebelling against "unethical" fundraising banners the Wikimedia Foundation wants to display on Wikipedia in coming weeks, calling them "guilt-tripping", "immoral", "This is not a fundraiser for Wikipedia – this is a fundraiser for the WMF"

Background: The Wikimedia Foundation has increased its revenue goal for this year to $175 million, a $20 million increase (actual hosting costs are about $2.5 million)

This happens every single year and every single year the WMF gives the middle finger to the community. The community won't/can't do anything, the WMF will just let them whine.

They are going to mess up a good thing. I don't know anyone who does not use this resource. Better ways to raise funds are available.
