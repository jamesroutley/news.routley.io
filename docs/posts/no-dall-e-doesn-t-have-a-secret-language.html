No, DALL-E doesn’t have a secret language

No, DALL-E doesn’t have a secret language. (or at least, we haven't found one yet) This viral DALL-E thread has some pretty astounding claims. But maybe the reason they’re so astounding is that, for the most part, they're not true. Thread 👇🧵 (1/15)

DALLE-2 has a secret language. "Apoploe vesrreaitais" means birds. "Contarra ccetnxniams luryca tanniounons" means bugs or pests. The prompt: "Apoploe vesrreaitais eating Contarra ccetnxniams luryca tanniounons" gives images of birds eating bugs. A thread (1/n)

Let’s start with some of the basic claims. 1)

says "Contarra ccetnxniams luryca tanniounons" means bugs or pests. This just seems wrong. Here's what I get if I put "Contarra ccetnxniams luryca tanniounons" into DALL-E - lots of different animals. (2/15)

The key to claims of a DALL-E "secret language" is that these terms apply across DALL-E prompts - including when used in more complex prompts, like asking DALL-E to output in other styles. But if I add “, 3d render” to the prompt I get sea-related things, not bugs. (3/15)

The prompts "Contarra ccetnxniams luryca tanniounons, cartoon" and ""Contarra ccetnxniams luryca tanniounons, painting" give me... grandmas?! (4/15)

2) How about the claim that “Apoploe vesrreaitais” means “birds” or “things that fly”? This does better. For the prompt “Apoploe vesrreaitais”, DALL-E does generate birds. (5/15)

If I try a cartoon, or a 3D render, DALL-E generates lots of bugs (some of which can fly) and no birds. My best guess? It’s random chance. Or just maybe (if you really press me) “Apoploe vesrreaitais” looks like a binomial name for some birds or bugs. (6/15)

3) Combining claims 1 and 2: does "Apoploe vesrreaitais eating Contarra ccetnxniams luryca tanniounons" give images of birds eating bugs? As you might expect from our previous results, this prompt definitely generates some birds, but I’m not sure there are any bugs. (7/15)

4)

says "Vicootes" means “vegetables”. Again, yes, "Vicootes" does give us some vegetable dishes. But: - “vicootes, cartoon” gives some weird characters - “vicootes, 3d render” gives objects - “vicootes, painting” gives flowers and landscapes (8/15)

To me this is all starting to look a lot more like stochastic, random noise, than a secret DALL-E language. (9/15)

Ok, let's dig a bit deeper. 5) Does the text in DALL-E images mean something?

uses the example of “Two whales talking about food, with subtitles" He then claims that using text from one of these pictures as a prompt will generate images of food. (10/15)

None of these pictures really have transcribable text, so I asked DALL-E to generate more whales until there was an image with text to copy. This is the first one DALL-E gave me. (11/15)

And look, prompting DALL-E with "Evve waeles" gave me a picture of a delicious dessert! But also - some footballers, some animals and a kettle? (12/15)

What do I think? "Evve waeles" is either nonsense, or a corruption of the word "whales". Giannis got lucky when his whales said "Wa ch zod rea" and that happened to generate pictures of food. (13/15)

CONCLUSION: To be fair to

, it's definitely weird that “Apoploe vesrreaitais” gives you birds, every time, despite seeming nonsense. So there's for sure something to this. (14/15)

But I don't think there's evidence there's a secret language across prompts - or that the text in DALL-E images means anything. And if there is evidence, I'm looking forward to being proven wrong!
