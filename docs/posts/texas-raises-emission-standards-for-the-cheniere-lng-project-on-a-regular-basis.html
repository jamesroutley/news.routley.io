Texas raises emission standards for the Cheniere LNG project on a regular basis

Cheniere’s massive LNG plant has exceeded permitted limits for emissions of pollutants such as soot, carbon monoxide, and volatile organic compounds (VOCs) hundreds of times since it started up.

The facility is now allowed to churn out some 353 tons per year of VOCs, double the limit set out in its original permit eight years ago.

U.S. President Joe Biden views expanding the LNG industry as a key tool for helping Europe reduce its energy dependence on Russia.

The Cheniere plant is regulated as a major pollution source under federal law.
