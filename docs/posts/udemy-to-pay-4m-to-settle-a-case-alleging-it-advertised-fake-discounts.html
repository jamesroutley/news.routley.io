Udemy to pay $4M to settle a case alleging it advertised fake discounts

Udemy has agreed to pay $4,000,000 to settle a case alleging it advertised fake discounts for its courses. Remember: using fake reference prices to run sales is illegal under the FTC Act and California's laws. 1/

The FTC rule says a former price needs to be “the actual, bona fide price at which the article was offered to the public on a regular basis for a reasonably substantial period of time.

California’s law (at issue in this case) says the former price needs to have been “the prevailing market price” within three months before the sale ads run. The consumers said Udemy violated that law by faking the references prices and running the "sales" for more than 90 days:

Class actions like this one have been common for years and aren’t going away. Yet brands continue to get it wrong and find themselves in trouble for it. This time, getting it wrong will cost Udemy $4M:

The case is Williams v. Udemy, Inc., San Diego Superior Court Case No. 37-2023--00003666-CU-BT-NC
