Making a watch from scratch

13

Greetings from Canada, self taught watchmaker here. Just finished making my first watch and wanted to share. An original design, made from scratch, by one person. The jewels, sapphire crystals, hairspring, mainspring and the strap were all that were purchased. Everything else was hand made. Feedback is always welcome so let me know what you think...

Case back with clickspring and winding works

Working on it in my free time, took over 3 years and countless hours to make. Naturally there were challenges along the way, but with a little patience and persistence it was a highly rewarding and enjoyable project. Still so much to learn and explore in the art of watchmaking and looking forward to making more

Close up of the escapement

Turning a 1.3mm diameter pinion to shape. The leaves were cut first using the lathe's milling attachment. With careful setup and plenty of oil the PP Thornton cutters left a nice finish

3rd wheel and it's pinion staked together

Cutting wheel teeth. The wheel is glued to the face of an arbor and placed in the collet. After setting up and a few cutting passes on the first tooth, the collet is rotated to the next tooth position and the process repeated until all teeth are cut

Pinions ready for heat treatment. The spares are good insurance, things don't always go as planned

Pinions in bluing tray for tempering

Polishing the pinion leaves by hand. Sorry I didn't to take an "after" pic of the polished surfaces

Burnishing the pinion pivots using a Jacot tool. Carbide burnisher used not shown
