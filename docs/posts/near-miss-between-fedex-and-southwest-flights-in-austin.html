Near-miss between FedEx and Southwest flights in Austin

NTSB investigation at Austin: FedEx 767 flight 1432 cleared to land on runway 18L. “Shortly before the FedEx aircraft was due to land, the controller cleared Southwest Flight 708 to depart from the same runway,” FAA says. FedEx aircraft got to 141 feet above runway per Fr24.

The Southwest flight started taking off with the FedEx flight behind it. Take off roll needs time to gain speed, so the FedEx flight caught up to the Southwest flight. At that moment, Flightradar24 suggests the two aircraft were vertically separated by 500 feet.

As the Southwest flight gained altitude, it banked to the right to avoid the FedEx aircraft, which turned to the left to increase horizontal separation. As they turned, vertical separation per Flightradar24 was about 670 feet.
