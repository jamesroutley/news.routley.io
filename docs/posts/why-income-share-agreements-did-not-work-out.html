Why income share agreements did not work out

Sean Linehan

@seanlinehan

I launched an Income Share Agreement (ISA) company in 2019. Our company survived, but our use of ISAs did not. Overall, I think the ISA experiment has failed and is not the revolution we hoped would transform training and education. Here's what I learned

ISAs tend to have significant adverse selection problems, stemming from two sources. First, the lack of skin in the game leads to very poor participant behavior. Participants haven't put anything down so it's easier to not complete the program.
